The gaps between needs and the system: exploring how people experience Indian government websites

A field audit series on Indian government portals - the ones people use when they've lost money, lost someone, or lost their way through a system that was supposed to help them. Each audit is filed from personal experience and documents every failure with a specific fix.

Auditing

What this is

A public audit series examining the UX of Indian government portals that people interact with during some of the most difficult moments of their lives. Filing a fraud complaint. Applying for a pension. Registering a death. Each audit goes through the actual flow, documents every problem, and proposes specific fixes that design and product teams can act on directly.

Who this is for

Design and product teams at NIC, MeitY, and state agencies. Journalists covering GovTech. Policy people working on digital public infrastructure. And anyone who thinks the standard for a government product should be higher than "it technically works." Every finding in this series comes from personally using the portal, not from screenshots alone.

Guiding beliefs

01Design for the worst day of someone's life, not the average day. If it works for someone in crisis, it works for everyone.
02The emotional state of the person using the product is part of the requirement. It belongs in the brief alongside technical specs.
03Shame is a UX problem. Any flow where the person might feel foolish or at fault must actively work against that feeling.
04Urgency is information. If time matters, the interface must say so before anything else on the page.
05Plain language is not dumbing down. It is precision. Bureaucratic jargon is imprecision dressed as formality.
06The government should never ask for something it already has.
07A partially completed form is a signal of a failing product, not a failing person.
08Every dead end must have a human door. If the digital process fails, the person must know exactly where to go next.
09Bad UX disproportionately harms the most vulnerable. The person with the most to lose is also the least equipped to navigate a broken system.
10Mobile-first is not a design choice in India. It is the only honest starting point.

These are architectural changes, not UI tweaks. Each one affects who can access the portal at all. Each one can be built using infrastructure the Indian government already owns.

01Replace username and password login with Aadhaar OTP
Every Indian who will use this portal already has an Aadhaar. Creating a separate account with a username, password, and a 30-minute OTP is redundant infrastructure layered on top of infrastructure that already exists. Aadhaar OTP login is live on DigiLocker, CoWIN, IRCTC, and UMANG. It would eliminate the registration wall, pre-fill name, address, state, and date of birth, remove the largest drop-off point in the flow, and fix the session expiry problem by tying the session to the Aadhaar token rather than the OTP.
Removes biggest drop-off pointUIDAI / Aadhaar API, existing govt infraAlready live on DigiLocker, CoWIN, IRCTC
02Connect to DigiLocker so the portal stops asking for what it already knows
An Aadhaar-authenticated person's name, address, state, and date of birth are already in DigiLocker. The portal asks for all of them again from scratch. This is not a form design failure. It is an integration failure. The UMANG app already demonstrates the right pattern: one sign-in, pre-filled data across services, correct only what is wrong. Connecting to the Aadhaar data layer via the existing UIDAI API would reduce the form to roughly 40% of its current length.
Reduces form length by ~40%UIDAI API, existingEliminates most frustrating fields
03Bhashini for full multilingual support, auto-detected not user-selected
The portal has a language toggle with partially machine-translated labels. That is not multilingual design. The Government of India built Bhashini specifically for this: full translation across 22 scheduled Indian languages. Every label, error message, status update, and confirmation should auto-detect the browser language and render natively - without the person doing anything. A Hindi-speaking pensioner from Bihar should not open this portal and immediately feel it was built for someone else.
Reaches every non-English speakerBhashini API, existing govt infraHighest-impact accessibility change possible
04Bhashini ASR voice input on the narration field
Most people filing here do not type fluently in English on a touchscreen. Narrating what happened in their voice is entirely natural. Typing it in a small textarea on a small phone is not. Bhashini's Automatic Speech Recognition supports live transcription in Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, and 18 other languages. A microphone icon on the narration field, available by default and not buried in accessibility settings, would meaningfully improve completion on the single most important field in the form.
Critical for elderly and low-literacy usersBhashini ASR, existing govt infraOne field change, outsized impact
05AI complaint classification from narration removes every confusing dropdown
Let the person narrate first. A language model reads "They called saying my SIM would be blocked unless I paid a fine to a CBI officer" and classifies it correctly - Fraud Call under Online Financial Fraud - populating all three dropdown fields in the backend automatically. The person never sees a dropdown. The complaint is more accurately classified than if a confused user guessed. The complaint is also more accurately classified than if a confused user guessed their own crime type.
AI classificationRemoves the hardest decision for the personImproves police data quality simultaneously
06WhatsApp as a filing channel, not just a notification channel
50 crore Indians use WhatsApp who have never filed a form on a government website. The government already has WhatsApp Business API access, used for CoWIN vaccine certificates and DigiLocker document sharing. Right now WhatsApp is only used to send one-way notifications from this portal. It should be a two-way filing channel. A person who cannot navigate cybercrime.gov.in messages "HELP" to a number. A bot in their language guides them through the same flow. Their complaint is filed. Their reference number arrives in the same chat.
Reaches 50 crore non-web usersWABA, existing govt API accessHighest reach expansion of all fixes
07Public recovery dashboard
Rs. 22,811 crore lost. Rs. 167 crore recovered. 2.18% recovery rate. This data exists in government reports but nowhere on the portal that processes the complaints. A public dashboard updated monthly - complaint volumes by state, average time to first response, amounts frozen versus recovered - would create accountability pressure. It also gives people honest expectations about recovery. Transparency is a choice. So is hiding this data.
Public trust and accountabilityForces internal improvementLow engineering effort, data already exists
08Dropout analytics so the team knows what is broken
There are no public dropout metrics, no field-level error frequency tracking, no session recording, no post-filing satisfaction survey. The team building this portal has no data on which step loses the most users, which field fails most often, or how long the form takes at the 90th percentile. A product flying blind stays broken. Step-level dropout tracking, field error frequency logging, and a two-question post-filing SMS survey would tell the team more in a month than years of internal reviews. NIC can build all of this into the existing infrastructure.
Without this, no other fix is measurableCan be built internallyEnables every future improvement to be validated

Session crashSub-category dropdown triggers a full page reload and kills the session
Selecting from the sub-category dropdown fires a synchronous form POST that reloads the entire page. This invalidates the CSRF session token. The user is silently logged out and taken to the login screen. All form data is gone. No warning. No recovery message. This happened during the filing of this audit.
Fix →Load sub-categories via AJAX, no page reload. Autosave all form fields to localStorage before any dropdown interaction. On re-login, detect the draft and restore automatically.
Session crashOTP expires in 30 minutes. The form takes longer.
The portal's own FAQ confirms the OTP is valid for 30 minutes. Filing a detailed complaint - pausing to find a transaction ID, narrating a two-hour scam call, typing in a second language on a slow phone - routinely takes longer. The portal compounds this by using OTP expiry as the session expiry. These are different things and should not be coupled.
Fix →Decouple session lifetime from OTP validity. Session minimum of two hours. Warn ten minutes before expiry. Autosave to localStorage every 30 seconds regardless of session state.
Session crashNo session heartbeat during passive reading or pausing
The idle timer is based on network requests, not keyboard input. A user who stops typing to look for a document, read an instruction, or collect themselves fires no network requests and drifts toward session death. A person staring at the screen deciding what to write is counted as idle by the server.
Fix →Silent keepalive API call every four minutes while the tab is focused. Reset idle timer on any user interaction: click, scroll, focus, or keydown. Never expire a session while a textarea has focus and the character count is actively increasing.
Session crashAfter a crash, re-login takes the user to the homepage, not their draft
When the session dies and the user logs back in, they land on the homepage. No draft is detected. No "resume where you left off" prompt appears. Starting from scratch is the only option.
Fix →On login, check localStorage for a draft matching this mobile number. If found: "You have an unfinished complaint from [time]. Resume?" Then restore all fields automatically.
Session crashNo warning before session expiry
No countdown. No banner. No prompt of any kind. The user discovers the session is gone only when they try to move to the next step and are redirected to login with all data lost.
Fix →Non-intrusive banner five minutes before expiry: "Your session expires in 5 minutes. Your draft is saved. Click anywhere to stay." One-click "Stay logged in" button.
Session crashSession does not extend during active narration typing
Idle time is calculated from network requests, not from keyboard activity. A user writing a long narration fires no network requests and can have their session expire while actively engaged with the most important field on the form. This punishes the people who take the most care.
Fix →Treat keyboard input on the narration field as activity. Send a debounced keepalive every 60 seconds during active typing. Never expire a session while a textarea has focus and the character count is rising.
Session crash"Best viewed in Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome" is still in the footer
This note signals that the site has browser-specific behaviour that was never properly resolved. The session crash on dropdown interaction may behave differently across browsers. Over 80% of Indian mobile users are on Chrome for Android or Samsung Internet, both of which exhibit the crash described above.
Fix →Run an automated cross-browser test suite. Prioritise Chrome Android, Samsung Internet, and Firefox Android. Remove the notice entirely. It is an admission of failure, not guidance.

StructuralYour employer is the gatekeeper to your own money

The bank verification loop
FunctionalBank KYC can stay "Under Process" for 20+ days with no resolution

StructuralThe portal's UX burden falls hardest on people with less digital familiarity
The CAPTCHA workaround requires knowing to type quickly, knowing to fill all other fields first, knowing to refresh immediately before submission. A 65-year-old applying for their first passport renewal doesn't know any of this. They fill the form carefully, take their time, hit submit — and the CAPTCHA fails. They try again. Again. They conclude the portal is broken. They pay an agent ₹500–₹2000 to do it for them. The portal's failure directly funds the agent economy around it.

The passport portal's problems are individually fixable. None require new infrastructure. They require engineering attention and the will to treat the applicant as someone who deserves a working product.

01Replace image CAPTCHA with OTP verification
The passport portal already requires a mobile number at registration. An OTP sent to that number is more secure than a CAPTCHA, eliminates the refresh-loop failure entirely, and is friendlier for users with visual impairments. Every other major Indian government portal — Aadhaar, DigiLocker, IRCTC, CoWIN — uses OTP. The CAPTCHA on passportindia.gov.in is an anachronism that actively harms the users it's meant to protect.
Eliminates most-reported failure on the portalOTP infrastructure already live for registration SMSMore secure than image CAPTCHA
02Autosave the application form after every section
The 8-step application has no autosave. A session timeout — at 20 minutes of inactivity — destroys all entered data. The fix is trivial: save the current form state to the server after every section is completed. If the session expires, the user returns to their draft on next login. DigiLocker already pre-fills most biographic fields — the application shouldn't require manual entry of data the government already has.
Eliminates data loss on timeoutDraft save is standard in any form systemDigiLocker integration for pre-fill
03Remove the 2-reschedule lifetime limit, or exempt portal-caused failures
The two-reschedule limit is a policy designed to prevent slot hoarding. But it penalises users for portal failures — CAPTCHA loops, session timeouts, payment gateway redirects — that are not the user's fault. The fix: either raise the limit to a reasonable number (4–5 reschedules per application), or add a mechanism where users can flag a portal-caused failure and have the reschedule count waived. MEA's own helpline can verify the portal failure via server logs.
Stops penalising users for portal failuresPolicy change required at MEA levelServer-log audit mechanism needed
04Pre-fill renewal applications from existing passport data
A passport renewal is, by definition, for a citizen whose data MEA already holds. The previous passport number, biographic details, address, and family information are all in the system. Renewal applications should pre-fill all unchanged fields from the existing record. The applicant confirms, updates what's changed (address, emergency contact), and submits. The 8-step form becomes a 2-step confirmation. DigiLocker holds the digital passport record — the pre-fill API is a natural extension of the existing DigiLocker integration.
Reduces application from 8 steps to 2 for renewalsExisting passport data already in MEA systemsDigiLocker integration already live
05Standardise DigiLocker acceptance at all PSKs
The portal says DigiLocker documents are accepted. PSK officers frequently don't. This is an implementation problem, not a policy problem — the policy is clear. MEA should enforce DigiLocker acceptance at all PSKs through officer training and a verification terminal at each counter that reads DigiLocker QR codes. The citizen who trusted the portal's word and brought only a phone should not be turned away.
Closes trust gap between portal promise and PSK realityDigiLocker legal validity already establishedOfficer training and verification terminal rollout

The complete step-by-step flow for renewing an Indian passport — from first visit to passport in hand — documented as it actually exists in April 2026.

1
Register on passportindia.gov.in
Name, DOB, email, mobile, CAPTCHA. CAPTCHA refreshes in background — enter it last, submit immediately. On success: verify email link + mobile OTP. Account created.
2
Login and navigate to "Apply for Fresh/Re-issue of Passport"
Select service type. For renewal: "Re-issue of Passport". Then select: Normal or Tatkal. Then select: 36 pages or 60 pages. Three separate choices before the form begins.
3
Fill 8-section application form
Sections: Passport Type, Applicant Details, Family Details, Present Address, Emergency Contact, Previous Passport, Other Details, Self Declaration. No autosave. Session timeout = start over. Previous passport data must be re-entered manually despite being in MEA's system.
4
Submit form — receive ARN (Application Reference Number)
On submit, an ARN is generated. Application is now in "Saved" state. No fee paid yet. Application can be edited at this stage.
5
Pay fee and schedule appointment
Navigate to "Pay and Schedule Appointment." Select PSK. Check slot availability (visible before payment — good). Pay via net banking/card/UPI. Risk: payment gateway CAPTCHA redirect loop. If it happens: log out, log in, navigate via View Saved Applications.
6

Bring: Aadhaar (original + self-attested copy), existing passport, address proof if changed. DigiLocker legally accepted — bring originals anyway in case the officer doesn't accept digital. Biometric capture. Document check. Police verification waived for renewals where address hasn't changed.
7

Portal shows status: Received → Under Process → Printed → Dispatched. Normal processing 3–7 business days from PSK visit. No SMS for most steps. Speed Post tracking number available on portal — often visible only after delivery.
8

Delivered to the address in the application. If you've moved, this is a problem — you cannot redirect. Missed delivery? You must collect from the Speed Post office within the holding period, or it's returned to the passport office. Redelivery process is manual and slow.

The passport portal uses MEA administrative terminology throughout — the vocabulary of the government officer processing the application, not the citizen filing it.

Service type labels
Jargon"Fresh Passport" vs "Re-issue" — not how people think about passports
The portal's primary service distinction is "Fresh Passport" (first time) vs "Re-issue" (renewal, name change, damaged, lost). "Re-issue" is an administrative term — citizens say "renew." The distinction is not obvious: a name change after marriage is a "re-issue," not a "fresh passport." A damaged passport is a "re-issue." A person who needs a replacement because their was stolen needs to know to select "Re-issue" — which most wouldn't.

Re-issue of Passport→Renew, replace, or update my existing passport
Passport categories
JargonECR / ECNR — explained nowhere on the application form
The application asks whether the applicant requires Emigration Check Required (ECR) or Not Required (ECNR) endorsement. ECR applies to those without a Class 10 certificate who may travel to 18 specific countries for work. ECNR applies to graduates and above. The form presents both options with no explanation. Selecting the wrong one can cause problems at immigration for foreign workers.
ECR→Emigration clearance required (for workers going to certain countries without 10th pass certificate)
ECNR→No emigration clearance needed (graduates and above — most applicants)

No warning that the CAPTCHA has expired. User submits the form. Server rejects CAPTCHA as invalid. Page reloads. All form data is cleared. The error message reads "Please enter correct captcha" — giving no indication that the CAPTCHA itself had expired invisibly.
