India's government websites handle some of the hardest moments in people's lives. This series looks at how.

A field audit series on Indian government portals - the ones people use when they've lost money, lost someone, or lost their way through a system that was supposed to help them. Each audit is filed from personal experience and documents every failure with a specific fix.

Independent research Filed from personal experience Open for comment

What this is

A public audit series examining the UX of Indian government portals that people interact with during some of the most difficult moments of their lives. Filing a fraud complaint. Applying for a pension. Registering a death. Each audit goes through the actual flow, documents every problem, and proposes specific fixes that design and product teams can act on directly.

Who this is for

Design and product teams at NIC, MeitY, and state agencies. Journalists covering GovTech. Policy people working on digital public infrastructure. And anyone who thinks the standard for a government product should be higher than "it technically works." Every finding in this series comes from personally using the portal, not from screenshots alone.

Guiding beliefs

01Design for the worst day of someone's life, not the average day. If it works for someone in crisis, it works for everyone.
02The emotional state of the person using the product is part of the requirement. It belongs in the brief alongside technical specs.
03Shame is a UX problem. Any flow where the person might feel foolish or at fault must actively work against that feeling.
04Urgency is information. If time matters, the interface must say so before anything else on the page.
05Plain language is not dumbing down. It is precision. Bureaucratic jargon is imprecision dressed as formality.
06The government should never ask for something it already has.
07A partially completed form is a signal of a failing product, not a failing person.
08Every dead end must have a human door. If the digital process fails, the person must know exactly where to go next.
09Bad UX disproportionately harms the most vulnerable. The person with the most to lose is also the least equipped to navigate a broken system.
10Mobile-first is not a design choice in India. It is the only honest starting point.

Surface fixes improve the experience. These architectural decisions change who can access justice at all. Each one is buildable using infrastructure the Indian government already owns and operates.

01Replace username and password login with Aadhaar OTP
Every Indian who will use this portal already has an Aadhaar. Creating a separate account with a username, password, and a 30-minute OTP is redundant infrastructure layered on top of infrastructure that already exists. Aadhaar OTP login is live on DigiLocker, CoWIN, IRCTC, and UMANG. It would eliminate the registration wall, pre-fill name, address, state, and date of birth, remove the largest drop-off point in the flow, and fix the 30-minute session expiry problem simultaneously by tying the session to the Aadhaar token.
Removes biggest drop-off pointUIDAI / Aadhaar API, existing govt infraAlready live on DigiLocker, CoWIN, IRCTC
02Connect to DigiLocker so the portal stops asking for what it already knows
An Aadhaar-authenticated person's name, address, state, and date of birth are already in DigiLocker. The portal asks for all of them again from scratch. This is not a form design failure. It is an integration failure. The UMANG app already demonstrates the right pattern: one sign-in, pre-filled data across services, correct only what is wrong. Connecting to the Aadhaar data layer via the existing UIDAI API would reduce the form to roughly 40% of its current length.
Reduces form length by ~40%UIDAI API, existingEliminates most frustrating fields
03Bhashini for full multilingual support, auto-detected not user-selected
The portal has a language toggle with partially machine-translated labels. That is not multilingual design. The Government of India built Bhashini specifically for this: production-quality translation across 22 scheduled Indian languages. Every label, error message, status update, and confirmation should auto-detect the browser language and render natively - without the person doing anything. A Hindi-speaking pensioner from Bihar should not open this portal and immediately feel it was built for someone else.
Reaches every non-English speakerBhashini API, existing govt infraHighest-impact accessibility change possible
04Bhashini ASR voice input on the narration field
The median person filing here is not someone who types fluently in English on a touchscreen. Narrating what happened in their voice is entirely natural. Typing it in a small textarea on a small phone is not. Bhashini's Automatic Speech Recognition supports live transcription in Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, and 18 other languages. A microphone icon on the narration field, available by default and not buried in accessibility settings, would double completion rates on that field alone.
Critical for elderly and low-literacy usersBhashini ASR, existing govt infraOne field change, outsized impact
05AI complaint classification from narration removes every confusing dropdown
Let the person narrate first. A language model reads "They called saying my SIM would be blocked unless I paid a fine to a CBI officer" and classifies it correctly - Fraud Call under Online Financial Fraud - populating all three dropdown fields in the backend automatically. The person never sees a dropdown. The complaint is more accurately classified than if a confused user guessed. Investigation data quality improves at the same time.
AI classificationRemoves the hardest decision for the personImproves police data quality simultaneously
06WhatsApp as a filing channel, not just a notification channel
50 crore Indians use WhatsApp who have never filed a form on a government website. The government already has WhatsApp Business API access, used for CoWIN vaccine certificates and DigiLocker document sharing. Right now WhatsApp is only used to send one-way notifications from this portal. It should be a two-way filing channel. A person who cannot navigate cybercrime.gov.in messages "HELP" to a number. A bot in their language guides them through the same flow. Their complaint is filed. Their reference number arrives in the same chat.
Reaches 50 crore non-web usersWABA, existing govt API accessHighest reach expansion of all fixes
07Public recovery dashboard
Rs. 22,811 crore lost. Rs. 167 crore recovered. 2.18% recovery rate. This data exists in government reports but nowhere on the portal that processes the complaints. A public dashboard updated monthly - complaint volumes by state, average time to first response, amounts frozen versus recovered - would create accountability pressure that no audit can. It also gives people honest expectations about outcomes. Transparency is a design decision. So is its absence.
Public trust and accountabilityForces internal improvementLow engineering effort, data already exists
08Dropout analytics so the team knows what is broken
There are no public dropout metrics, no field-level error frequency tracking, no session recording, no post-filing satisfaction survey. The team building this portal has no data on which step loses the most users, which field fails most often, or how long the form takes at the 90th percentile. A product flying blind stays broken. Step-level dropout tracking, field error frequency logging, and a two-question post-filing SMS survey would generate more actionable data in one month than years of committee reviews. NIC can build this into the existing infrastructure.
Without this, no other fix is measurableCan be built internallyEnables every future improvement to be validated

Every step a fraud victim takes, from the first second on the homepage to reading a status update months later. Documented as experienced, in sequence.

1
Homepage
No acknowledgment that the user is in the right place. No emotional grounding. The first visible element after the header is a legal disclaimer that must be accepted before anything else. The most time-sensitive action on the site, calling 1930 to freeze a transaction, does not appear above the fold anywhere. Recovery drops from 60% if reported within one hour to under 15% after 24 hours.
Critical1930 is not mentioned above the fold
Every minute the user spends navigating a confusing homepage is a minute the money is moving. This number must be the first thing anyone sees on the site, in a sticky, high-contrast banner that cannot be dismissed.
No urgent guidance visible→Sticky top banner: "Lost money in last 4 hours? Call 1930 right now to freeze the transaction before it moves."
ShameNo emotional acknowledgment on entry
Not one sentence says: you are in the right place, this is not your fault. Only 1 in 10 cyber crimes gets reported in India. Shame is the primary barrier. The homepage has a responsibility to address that in the first three seconds.
Add before anything else →"You're in the right place. Over 19 lakh people reported here last year. This is not your fault."
2
Legal disclaimer gate
Before seeing any form, before receiving any guidance, the user must read and accept a full-page legal disclaimer. For someone whose money was stolen an hour ago, this communicates one thing: the government is protecting itself before it protects you.
Critical"I Accept" is the very first action on the site
A legal disclaimer at the entrance serves no function that a checkbox at the review step does not serve equally well. It is pure friction at the worst possible moment. Let people in immediately. Move the disclaimer to just before submission.
Disclaimer page → "I Accept" → then see the form→Open the form directly. Disclaimer checkbox at the review step, one click before submission.
3
Category selection
Two buttons: "Report Crime related to Women/Child" and "Report Other Cybercrimes." A person scammed by a fake CBI officer over WhatsApp does not know which applies. Many people who have been defrauded do not even think of what happened to them as a "cyber crime."
CriticalTaxonomy built for police routing, not for victims
The two-way split is a legal classification serving internal routing, not user comprehension. The result is wrong categorisation, incorrect routing, and weaker complaints. Users should see their situation described in plain language, not a legal category.
"Report Crime related to Women/Child" vs "Report Other Cybercrimes"
→Chips with plain labels: "Phone or WhatsApp scam" / "UPI or bank fraud" / "Fake job or investment" / "Account hacked" / "Blackmail or threats" / "Something else"
4
Registration wall
Full account creation is required before a single complaint field can be filled. Name, email, mobile, password, and an OTP that expires in 30 minutes. An elderly person on a low-end phone, under emotional stress, must complete all of this before describing what happened.
CriticalAccount creation forced before complaint filing
The portal should let users begin filing immediately and create the account silently in the background, linked to their mobile number. No username. No password. No email verification before the form.
Create account → verify email → then file→Mobile OTP only → start filing immediately → account created automatically with the reference number
UX gapNo explanation of why an account is needed
A single line - "Your account lets you track your complaint and add evidence later" - would reduce abandonment significantly by making the registration feel purposeful rather than bureaucratic.
5
Sub-category dropdown
Selecting from the sub-category dropdown triggers a full page reload that invalidates the session token, logging the user out instantly. All form data is gone. No warning. No recovery screen. Experienced firsthand while filing.
Session crashDropdown selection logs the user out and destroys all data
The sub-category change fires a synchronous POST that reloads the page and resets the CSRF token. The user lands on the login screen with no explanation. Everything typed is gone. This is the most damaging bug on the entire site.
Select sub-category → page reload → session gone → login screen → empty form
Fix →Load sub-categories via AJAX, no page reload. Autosave all fields to localStorage before any dropdown interaction. Restore draft automatically on re-login.
6
The complaint form
A single long page with no progress indicator, no autosave, a 30-minute session timeout with no warning, a mandatory field for Father's or Husband's name, and a field labelled "Narrate the incident in brief." For elderly users typing slowly or pausing to find documents, every one of these is a reason to give up.
Session crashNo autosave. Timeout kills everything without warning.
The person most likely to take a long time on this form is an elderly user typing slowly, possibly in a second language, pausing to find a bank statement. That person is also most likely to lose everything to a timeout. Autosave to localStorage every 30 seconds costs nothing.
Session expires → all data gone → start over→Autosave every 30 seconds. Visible "Draft saved" indicator. Warn 5 minutes before timeout. Restore on re-login.
CriticalNo progress indicator
No sense of how long this will take or how much remains. On a mobile screen, an endless scrolling form with no visible end is one of the most reliable ways to cause abandonment.
Single long scrolling page, no indicator→Step 1 of 4: What happened / Step 2: Your details / Step 3: Evidence / Step 4: Review and submit

When filing more than 72 hours after the incident, the portal presents a mandatory dropdown: "Select reason for delay." Options include "Was busy in other works." A victim paralysed by shame, caring for a sick relative, or simply unaware this portal existed must select the closest label for their failure. This field has no investigative value and causes real harm.

Users encounter requests for UTR numbers and transaction IDs mid-form with no warning. A 30-second checklist screen before the form opens prevents this abandonment entirely.

No upfront guidance on accepted formats. The error message appears only after a failed upload attempt. No way to add evidence after the complaint is filed, which means a victim who finds a call recording later must start an entirely new complaint.
Critical"Invalid file format" with no prior guidance
Most users do not know what format their phone screenshots are. The result is that they give up and file without evidence, which weakens the complaint and slows down investigation.
[Upload field with no guidance] → "Invalid file format"→Show before the field: "Upload JPG, PNG or PDF. Max 5MB. Your phone screenshots are usually JPG and will work."

8
Confirmation screen
A 14-digit reference number is generated and sent by SMS. This part works. What follows does not. The confirmation page says nothing about what happens next, and the reference number is displayed in plain text with no copy button.
CriticalNo "what happens next" explanation after filing
After 30 or more minutes of emotionally draining form-filling, the user sees "Your complaint has been registered" and a reference number. No timeline. No next step. No reassurance. This drives unnecessary calls to 1930 and leaves the user feeling exactly as abandoned as before they filed.
"Complaint registered. Ref: XXXX"→Three-step visual: Received and saved → Police review within 24 hours → You will get an SMS at every update. Plus: "What you experienced was a crime. Not your fault."
UX gapReference number has no copy button
A 14-character alphanumeric string in plain text. Elderly users attempting to write it down frequently make transcription errors. One-tap copy is the minimum acceptable standard for any reference number on any platform.

Full login required to check status. Labels use police shorthand. No proactive SMS when the case moves. Users must actively check, and when they do, they often cannot interpret what they are looking at.

"Disposed," "Forwarded to SP Zone 3," "Pending at District Level." "Disposed" sounds like the complaint was thrown away. No tooltip, no explanation, no next step for any status value on the entire page.

The person who filed may be elderly and unable to log in repeatedly. A family member helping them should be able to check status using just the reference number and a mobile OTP.

A plain-language SMS at each stage change would eliminate anxiety, reduce calls to 1930, and is the bare minimum of what "public service" should mean. It costs almost nothing.

The form actively destroys user data. These technical failures have direct human cost. Each one means a victim starting over, or stopping entirely.

Session crashSub-category dropdown triggers a full page reload and kills the session
Selecting from the sub-category dropdown fires a synchronous form POST that reloads the entire page. This invalidates the CSRF session token. The user is silently logged out and taken to the login screen. All form data is gone. No warning. No recovery message. This was experienced firsthand while filing this audit.
Fix →Load sub-categories via AJAX, no page reload. Autosave all form fields to localStorage before any dropdown interaction. On re-login, detect the draft and restore automatically.
Session crashOTP expires in 30 minutes. The form takes longer.
The portal's own FAQ confirms the OTP is valid for 30 minutes. Filing a detailed complaint - pausing to find a transaction ID, narrating a two-hour scam call, typing in a second language on a slow phone - routinely takes longer. The portal compounds this by using OTP expiry as the session expiry. These are different things and should not be coupled.
Fix →Decouple session lifetime from OTP validity. Session minimum of two hours. Warn ten minutes before expiry. Autosave to localStorage every 30 seconds regardless of session state.
Session crashNo session heartbeat during passive reading or pausing
The idle timer is based on network requests, not keyboard input. A user who stops typing to look for a document, read an instruction, or collect themselves fires no network requests and drifts toward session death. A person staring at the screen deciding what to write is counted as idle by the server.
Fix →Silent keepalive API call every four minutes while the tab is focused. Reset idle timer on any user interaction: click, scroll, focus, or keydown. Never expire a session while a textarea has focus and the character count is actively increasing.
Session crashAfter a crash, re-login takes the user to the homepage, not their draft
When the session dies and the user logs back in, they land on the homepage. No draft is detected. No "resume where you left off" prompt appears. Starting from scratch is the only option. This is the second injury after the crash itself.
Fix →On login, check localStorage for a draft matching this mobile number. If found: "You have an unfinished complaint from [time]. Resume?" Then restore all fields automatically.
Session crashNo warning before session expiry
No countdown. No banner. No prompt of any kind. The user discovers the session is gone only when they try to move to the next step and are redirected to login with all data lost.
Fix →Non-intrusive banner five minutes before expiry: "Your session expires in 5 minutes. Your draft is saved. Click anywhere to stay." One-click "Stay logged in" button.
Session crashSession does not extend during active narration typing
Idle time is calculated from network requests, not from keyboard activity. A user writing a long narration fires no network requests and can have their session expire while actively engaged with the most important field on the form. This specifically punishes the most thorough, most vulnerable users.
Fix →Treat keyboard input on the narration field as activity. Send a debounced keepalive every 60 seconds during active typing. Never expire a session while a textarea has focus and the character count is rising.
Session crash"Best viewed in Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome" is still in the footer
This note signals that the site has browser-specific behaviour that was never properly resolved. The session crash on dropdown interaction may behave differently across browsers. Over 80% of Indian mobile users are on Chrome for Android or Samsung Internet, both of which exhibit the crash described above.
Fix →Run an automated cross-browser test suite. Prioritise Chrome Android, Samsung Internet, and Firefox Android. Remove the notice entirely. It is an admission of failure, not helpful guidance.

After filing, there is no mention of whether the scammer's account has been flagged, whether any funds have been frozen, or whether the bank has been notified. Giving the victim even partial information about what their complaint triggered reduces the helplessness that shame feeds on.

DesignLanguage first, form never
The conversation detects language from the first reply and switches entirely. Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali - all via Bhashini. The person never sees an English form.
DesignOne question at a time
The bot asks one thing, waits, moves on. A 65-year-old who has never filed anything online can do this. They already know how to reply to a WhatsApp message.
DesignScreenshot replaces UTR
The bot asks for a screenshot from the gallery. The backend reads the transaction ID, amount, date, and recipient from the image. No one needs to know what a UTR number is.
DesignNarration classifies the complaint
The person describes what happened in their own words. AI classifies it correctly and fills the backend form invisibly. No dropdown. No category selection. No chance of misfiling.
DesignSummary before filing
Before anything is submitted, the bot shows a plain-language summary and asks for confirmation. The person can correct any detail. Just: does this look right?
DesignReference number stays in the chat
Confirmation arrives in the same thread. No login needed to find it. Future status updates arrive in the same thread too.
InfrastructureWhat it takes to build this
WhatsApp Business API - already held by the government for CoWIN and DigiLocker. Bhashini for language detection and translation. A structured LLM prompt for narration classification. An image reader for transaction screenshots. The NCRP backend API to file the complaint. Nothing here requires new infrastructure. It requires connecting what already exists.
